IRMA REGISTRATION

IRMA privacy policy

This text briefly describes the privacy policy for (phase 1 of) the pilot project IRMA. This project involves experimental use of attribute-based identity management via smart cards. The text consists of the following parts.

  1. Goals
  2. Relevant parties and scope
  3. The IRMA backoffice
  4. Duration

1. Goals

The overall goal of the IRMA project is to develop and evaluate an experimental framework for privacy-friendly attribute-based identity management using smart cards. To a restricted group of people in the academic context in the Netherlands the possibility will be offered to participate and obtain a smart card that can contain digital attributes (properties) that hold for the Cardholder. These attributes can be obtained from an Issuer, and can be checked by a Relying Party in an authentication test as part of a (unspecified) transaction.

2. Relevant parties and scope

The IRMA project is in phase 1 a (non-profit, informal) cooperation between:

  • the Digital Security research group of the Radboud University Nijmegen, hereafter RU;
  • Surfnet;
  • TNO-ICT Groningen, hereafter TNO.

The IRMA project involves (personal) data storage by four parties:

  • a) The Cardholder participating voluntarily in the IRMA project, having data on his/her own IRMA card (and possibly historical transaction information at some personal external logging and inspection device, like a PC);
  • b) RU running the IRMA Backoffice, holding Cardholder information;
  • c) An Issuer that authenticates Cardholders and delivers attributes to their cards;
  • d) A Relying Party that verifies and uses attributes on IRMA smart cards. In phase 1 of the IRMA project a Relying Party can, in principle, read all attributes. (Restrictions will be imposed in later phases.)

This privacy policy covers party (b); parties (c) and (d) will be responsible for their own privacy policy. Party (a) will be responsible for the management of his/her own personal data.

The names `IRMA' and `IRMA project' may also be used in follow-up activities. Therefor, the current text addresses "phase 1" of the project. A later phase may involve different parties and different roles, and thus requires an adaptation of this privacy policy. Whenever the terms `IRMA' and `IRMA project' are used in this document without further qualification, they refer to this phase 1.

3. The IRMA backoffice

The IRMA backoffice will operate at, and under the responsability of, the Digital Security group of the Radboud University Nijmegen (RU): it will remain within the university infrastructure, and will not be realised via the cloud, or via subcontractors. It will be used for the application, production, hand-over, card-loss, and similar administrative tasks during the life-cycle of IRMA smart cards. Additionally, the Backoffice will keep a log of all transactions. The Digital Security group will take appropriate technical and organisational security measures in order to protect personal data from being lost and from any type of unlawful processing. Taking account of the state of technology and the cost of implementing them, these measures will provide an appropriate level of security in view of the risks associated with such processing and the nature of the data being protected. Moreover, the data wil be used only for the purposes described below.

There will be no third-party access to the data items. Derived anonimised statistical information may be disclosed (like numbers of participants, card-losses, errors, etc.) in reports and publications about the IRMA project.

When a prospective Cardholder agrees to participate in the IRMA project and explicitly consents to the data processing involved, the following data will be stored in the IRMA backoffice, as part of the application procedure for his/her personal IRMA card.

  • (a) First name + Last name
  • (b) Email address
  • (c) Photo of the Cardholder
  • (d) uid@institution identifier
  • (e) interaction log
    • date+time
    • log entry

Ad (a) + (b): The name and email address will be used solely for communication purposes.

Ad (c): The Photo of the Cardholder will be printed on the outside of the card, and will not be present inside the (chip on the) smart card. The Photo will be compared to a photo on a valid identity document at card hand-over. Afterwards, the Photo will be kept in the database for consistency checks, like in fraud detection, and in re-issuance of a card after claimed card-losses.

Ad (d): The uid@institution is the identifier that Surfnet uses to identify students and academic staff members in the Netherlands. It will be incorporated in a "root credential" that is installed on an empty card, before card hand-over. This root credential may be read and used by both Issuers and Relying Parties.

Ad (e): The interaction log involves date, time, and source IP-adres of a request for an IRMA card. These data will be used solely for performance evaluation, problem solving, and fraud detection.

During the course of the project new functionality may be introduced that requires additional processing of personal data. This will only happen with explicit consent of the data subject, and will result in an explicit description of the new purpose in this privacy policy.

4. Duration

The IRMA project (phase 1) will run during at most two (academic) years, namely from 1 sept. 2013 to 1 sept. 2015. All personal data will be deleted or anonymised soon as they are not necessary any longer for the purpose of the processing activity. Such deletion will surely happen within at most 6 months after termination of phase 1.

If there will be subsequent phases of the IRMA project, and if personal data from phase 1 can be re-used in these later phases, this will only happen with explicit consent of the data subject.
